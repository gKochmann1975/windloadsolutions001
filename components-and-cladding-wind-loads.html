What are Components and Cladding Wind Loads?

Components and cladding (C&C) wind loads are the design wind pressures applied to building envelope elements that receive wind pressure directly but do not contribute to the overall lateral resistance of the structure. Unlike the Main Wind Force Resisting System (MWFRS) which carries loads to the foundation, C&C elements transfer wind forces to the structural frame through local connections.

C&C elements include windows, doors, curtain walls, wall panels, roof panels, sheathing, fasteners, and all other exterior components. These elements experience significantly higher wind pressures than MWFRS because they have smaller tributary areas, experience peak local pressure fluctuations, and are subject to concentrated pressures at building edges and corners.

Why C&C Loads are Higher

  • Smaller Effective Wind Areas: Smaller components see higher pressure coefficients per ASCE 7
  • Peak Pressure Effects: Local pressure peaks that don't occur simultaneously across entire structure
  • Edge and Corner Zones: Concentrated wind pressures at building discontinuities
  • Less Spatial Averaging: Smaller tributary areas mean less pressure averaging

Common C&C Design Applications

  • Windows and Doors: Glazing selection, frame design, and anchor spacing
  • Roof Sheathing: Panel thickness and fastener spacing requirements
  • Wall Cladding: Panel design and attachment to structural frame
  • Roofing Membranes: Insulation and membrane fastening patterns
  • Solar Panels: Ballast or attachment requirements for rooftop arrays
